My Nigerian Favourite Dish

Nigerian Jollof Rice

Jollof rice, a spicy and traditional dish of West African origin, is a great place to start. Although it's prepared differently in various countries, we'll walk you through the best way to make a Nigerian version of this classic dish. Before you know it, your jollof rice will be ready to serve and share with friends.

Ingredients Measurement
rice 2 cups
Onion 1
Pepper 1 red bell
Chooped tomatoes 500ml
tomato Puree 100g
oil 100ml
Salt to taste
Maggi 2 cube
stalk 1 cup
    Direction
  1. Blend your tomatoes, scotch bonnet, and tatashe together

    2. Chop your onions and keep them aside

  2. Put your rice into a bowl and soak with hot water.

    3. Allow this to soak for 10-15 minutes. Then, wash with warm water and keep aside, or parboil your rice and wash. This is important as you will get rid of excess starch in the rice

  3. Heat up your oil

    4. Put your oil in a pot and allow it toe heat up

  4. Add your chopped onions and allow them to fry

    5. Be sure not to burn the onion in the process

  5. Add and fry your blended ingredients and purée
  6. Add your maggi, garlicm ginger, curry thyme and salt.

    7. Mix it all together.Be careful with your maggi as you do not want it too salty(q maggi cubes for a cup of rice). add your stock or water, and mix too. Taste to adjust to any seasoning

  7. Pour your rice into the pot and mix together.

    8. Make sure it is covered in the tomato sauce

  8. Cook on low medium heat

    9. Check your rice after at least 10 minutes, using a wooden spatula to dip into the rice.The helps getting the sauce to get the bottom of the pot, so that it does not start burning when the rice is not cooked.

  9. Cook on low medium heat

    10. Do not overcook your rice or get your rice too soggy.When rice is soft, lower the heat and allow to simmer so that water will get completely dry.

  10. Serve the rice.

    11. Serve with fried plantain and your choice of meat, if desired.
